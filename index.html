The Story

I started in marketing.
Curiosity made me a strategist.

My career began in marketing at a B2B technology company. Within six months of joining, my manager departed — and I found myself solely responsible for the organisation's entire content and marketing operation.

That experience taught me that content without strategy is decoration. Every piece of content I published needed to answer a question: who is this for, what does it need to do, and how will we know it worked?

I built SEO strategies from research, not assumption. I studied what the data said about audience behaviour and changed course when it told me to. I grew social communities, produced weekly reports for leadership, and learned how to translate creative decisions into business outcomes.

I then deliberately went deeper into data analysis — building Python projects, SQL pipelines, and Power BI dashboards — not to become a data engineer, but to ensure that my creative instincts were always grounded in evidence. A strategist who can read data is significantly more effective than one who cannot.
