The Problem

A cybersecurity company with no digital presence

When I joined StrongBox IT as a Digital Marketing Executive, the company had no LinkedIn page, no Instagram, no YouTube channel, and no structured content strategy. The website existed — but it had no SEO foundation. There was no editorial calendar, no keyword strategy, and content decisions were based on instinct rather than data.

The brief was simple but wide open: build the brand's digital presence from zero and drive meaningful traffic and leads through content.

The Diagnosis

Finding the gaps before creating anything

Before writing a single word, I ran a full content and SEO audit. I used SEMrush and Ubersuggest to identify keywords with high search volume and low keyword difficulty — terms like penetration testing services and penetration testing companies in India where StrongBox IT could realistically compete and rank.

I also audited every piece of content the company had previously published to identify what was redundant, what was missing, and where we were duplicating effort without adding value.

Key insight: The existing content was scattered and undifferentiated — no clear audience targeting, no keyword alignment, and heavy repetition across service pages. The opportunity was to build a systematic, structured content engine rather than produce more of the same.
SEMrush Ubersuggest Google Search Console GA4 Google Trends
The Strategy

A weekly content system built around data, not opinions

I designed a repeatable weekly content system covering both website SEO and social media — two parallel tracks that reinforced each other.

Website — SEO content cadence

1 blog + 2 landing pages per week. Each landing page targeted a specific city or region (Oman, Dubai, India) with local keyword intent. I built the H1/H2/H3 keyword architecture for every page, briefed the content writer, reviewed all output, and published directly via WordPress and Elementor.

Social — editorial calendar system

Built a weekly content calendar across LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube. I selected topics using Google Trends and cybersecurity news, decided the content format for each post — carousel, video, puzzle, poll, newsletter, blog snippet — and managed the full creative pipeline from brief to publish.

For social media, I made a deliberate strategic choice to lead with awareness and education rather than promotion. Cybersecurity audiences respond to useful, trust-building content — so the calendar prioritised insights, threat trends, and practical tips over product announcements.

The Result

Every channel grew. Every number was tracked.

40%
Organic traffic growth — from approximately 500 to 700+ monthly sessions through systematic keyword-led content publishing and on-page SEO optimisation across 12+ service pages.
25%
Inbound lead growth — driven by location-specific landing pages built around high-intent local search behaviour identified through GA4 and Search Console.
5,000
LinkedIn followers from zero — the company page did not exist before I joined. Built to 5,000 in 12 months through consistent educational content and data-led format decisions.
500+
YouTube subscribers from a channel I created from scratch — alongside active Instagram and Facebook presences, also built from zero.
What made this work: Every decision was tracked and adjusted weekly. When LinkedIn analytics showed that educational carousel posts outperformed promotional content by 5x in impressions, I rebuilt the entire social strategy around that finding immediately — rather than waiting for a monthly review.
